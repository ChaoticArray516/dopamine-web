Fake shopping sites let you browse, add to cart, and check out — without ever paying. The [dopamine sites directory](/sites) includes several of them in the [fake shopping category](/sites/fake-shopping), and they are more popular than you might expect.

## Why fake shopping feels good

Real shopping releases dopamine in anticipation of reward. Fake shopping triggers the same anticipation loop — browsing pretty things, comparing options, clicking "buy" — but removes the financial consequence. You get the buzz without the bill.

## FoodNeverComes

[FoodNeverComes](/sites/foodnevercomes) is a perfect example. You can fill a cart with gourmet groceries, beautiful produce, and desserts. The site looks real, the prices look real, but nothing ever ships.

For most people, fake shopping is a harmless distraction. It can even be a useful alternative when you feel the urge to impulse-buy. For more mood-boosting alternatives, read about [dopamine decor](/dopamine-decor) or browse the full [directory](/sites).
